UNIT I: Introduction to Underwater Acoustics (9 hours): Covers sound propagation in ocean water, thermoclines, sound velocity profiles (SVP) in deep/shallow water, sound attenuation (absorption, scattering, reverberation), and Snell's law. Includes laser communication.
UNIT II: Underwater Navigation & Aiding Sensors (9 hours): Details navigational sensors including accelerometers, Fiber Optic Gyroscopes (FOGs), and Ring Laser Gyroscopes (RLG), along with Attitude Heading and Reference Systems (AHRS), IMU, Doppler Velocity Log, and calibration methods.
UNIT III: Acoustic Positioning Systems (9 hours): Covers vehicle positioning techniques: Short Base Line (SBL), Super Short Base Line (SSBL), and Long Base Line (LBL) configurations and operating principles.
UNIT IV: Subsea Vehicle Navigation (9 hours): Focuses on underwater navigation solutions, challenges, and types, covering aided navigation systems and Inertial Navigational systems.
UNIT V: Operational Planning and Deployment (9 hours): Focuses on tethered vehicle deployment, AUV/ROV search operations, data acquisition, and acoustic transponder recovery.
